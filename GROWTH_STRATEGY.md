# Growth Strategy

## Core Philosophy

**Growth is not marketing. Growth is product + data.**

Most startups think: "Build product, then do marketing."

We think: "Build product for growth. Use data to find what works. Scale aggressively."

---

## Growth Model

### Phase 1: Seeding (0-1K DAU)

**Goal:** Product-market fit with early adopters

**Strategy:**
- **Closed beta with 500 hand-selected die-hard music fans**
  - Recruited from Jacob's network, label connections, producer network
  - High-intent users (they WANT to be here)
  - Incentivize invites (small rewards, credits, exclusive early access)
  - Goal: Understand what hooks them, what doesn't

- **Measure obsessively:**
  - Which types of users stick?
  - Which features drive the most engagement?
  - What's the referral coefficient? (Each user brings ___ friends)
  - Where does churn happen? (Why do people leave?)

- **Iterate ruthlessly:**
  - Weekly product updates based on data
  - Remove features that don't move retention/engagement
  - Double down on what works

**Success:** 1K DAU with 30%+ W1 retention (1 in 3 users come back next week)

---

### Phase 2: Network Effects (1K-10K DAU)

**Goal:** Prove that invites drive growth (viral coefficient > 1)

**Strategy:**
- **Ambassador program**
  - Recruit 50-100 musicians, producers, music critics, tastemakers
  - Give them 1-2 weeks exclusive early access
  - Ask them to share with their networks
  - Track which ambassadors drive the most signups
  - Scale investment toward high-impact ambassadors

- **Seeded communities**
  - Partner with 5-10 Reddit communities (r/indieheads, r/hiphopheads, etc.)
  - Not spammy promotion; genuine engagement
  - Top contributors get ambassador status
  - They bring their friends organically

- **Referral mechanics**
  - Make invites frictionless (one-click share to WhatsApp, iMessage, etc.)
  - Reward inviters and invitees (exclusive early features, badges, etc.)
  - Track conversion: What % of invites convert to active users?
  - Goal: Referral coefficient > 1.2 (each user brings 1.2+ friends)

- **Measure obsessively:**
  - Referral coefficient by source (which ambassadors convert best?)
  - Invite-to-signup rate
  - Viral loop duration (how long from invite to activation to referral?)
  - LTV by cohort (which source brings most valuable users?)

**Success:** 10K DAU with referral coefficient proving growth momentum

---

### Phase 3: Partnership-Driven Growth (10K-100K DAU)

**Goal:** Leverage network to accelerate growth

**Strategy:**
- **Label partnerships**
  - Approach 10-20 major and indie labels
  - Offer: Exclusive drops, artist AMAs, early album access
  - Ask in return: Promote to fans (emails, social, artist announcements)
  - Measure: Which labels drive most high-value user signups?

- **Talent manager / artist partnerships**
  - Identify 50-100 emerging artists (UTA roster, others)
  - Offer: Free marketing to fans, community engagement, exclusive content platform
  - Ask: Cross-promote to their fanbases
  - Measure: Artist follower to app signups conversion

- **Media partnerships**
  - Partner with music publications (Pitchfork, The Needle Drop, etc.)
  - Cross-promotion on both platforms
  - Featured content exchanges
  - Measure: Traffic/signups from each partner

- **University seeding**
  - Identify top 20 universities (music scene hot spots)
  - Recruit 1-2 student ambassadors per campus
  - Fund campus events (listening parties, artist talks)
  - Goal: Organic adoption from college-aged users (high network density)

- **Influencer/Creator seeding**
  - Identify 100+ music YouTubers, TikTokers, Spotify playlisters
  - Offer: Featured playlists, exclusive content, revenue share (future)
  - Ask: Cross-promote, include app in videos/bio links
  - Measure: Click-through rate, signup attribution

**Monetization integration:**
- Bundle partnerships with exclusive experiences
- Example: Label A promotes app to 500K fans, gets exclusive drop first
- Example: Influencer promotes, gets commission on ticket sales through app

**Success:** 100K DAU, with 30%+ of growth from partnerships

### Phase 4: Category Ownership (100K-1M DAU)

**Goal:** Become synonymous with "music social"

**Strategy:**
- **Press & awards**
  - Target tech + music publications
  - Share growth story (data, user testimonials, community highlights)
  - Goal: "Music Platform is the Letterboxd of music" becomes the narrative

- **Organic momentum**
  - At 100K DAU, word-of-mouth accelerates
  - Network effects take over (more users = more content = more discovery)
  - Referrals become primary growth driver

- **Product excellence focus**
  - Shift from growth hacking to product quality
  - International expansion (UK, EU, APAC)
  - New features (live events, community features, creator tools)

- **Ecosystem strength**
  - 100+ official artist profiles
  - 20+ major label partnerships
  - 500+ ambassador network
  - Events, exclusive drops, community experiences
  - Users see platform as essential for music discovery

**Success:** 1M DAU, category owned, sustainable unit economics

## Data-Driven Growth Tactics

### 1. Cohort Analysis

**What it means:** Track groups of users by signup date and measure their behavior over time.

**Example:**
- Users from Week 1 (hand-selected beta): 40% D7 retention, 2.5 referrals/user
- Users from Week 2 (ambassador wave): 35% D7 retention, 1.8 referrals/user
- Users from Week 3 (organic): 25% D7 retention, 1.2 referrals/user

**Decision:** Double down on Week 1 and Week 2 strategies. Week 3 strategy isn't working.

### 2. Source Attribution

**What it means:** Track where each user came from and measure their lifetime value.

**Example:**
- Direct (word-of-mouth): 45% of users, 40% D7 retention, $15 LTV
- Referral (friends): 35% of users, 35% D7 retention, $12 LTV
- Ambassador (musicians): 15% of users, 50% D7 retention, $20 LTV
- Paid (ads): 5% of users, 15% D7 retention, $3 LTV

**Decision:** Abandon paid ads. Invest more in ambassadors. Optimize for referrals.

### 3. Feature Usage Correlation

**What it means:** Which features correlate with retention and referrals?

**Example:**
- Users who share 3+ songs in first week: 60% D7 retention
- Users who comment: 55% D7 retention
- Users who follow 5+ people: 50% D7 retention
- Users who only browse: 20% D7 retention

**Decision:** Make sharing and commenting easier (fewer clicks, better UX). Measure impact.

### 4. A/B Testing Priority

**Never run tests without a hypothesis:**

1. Share button placement: "Moving share button to home screen increases shares by 5%"
2. Onboarding flow: "Asking users to follow 5 people increases D7 retention by 10%"
3. Feed ordering: "Showing trending songs first increases comment rate by 8%"
4. Notification timing: "Digest at 6 PM gets 3x open rate vs. real-time"

**Discipline:** Only run one test at a time. Measure for 1 week. Decide.

### 5. Viral Loop Optimization

**The loop:** Invite → Signup → Activation → Referral

**Measure each step:**
- Invite rate: How many users invite per week? (Currently 30%)
- Invite-to-signup: What % of invites convert to signups? (Currently 25%)
- Activation: What % of signups become active? (Currently 60%)
- Time-to-referral: How long until new user invites friends? (Currently 8 days)

**Optimization:** If activation is the bottleneck, redesign onboarding. If time-to-referral is high, surface sharing earlier.

## CAC vs. LTV Framework

**We're not paying for users, we're earning them.**

| Channel | CAC | LTV | Payback | Worth? |
|---------|-----|-----|---------|--------|
| Hand-recruited (beta) | $0 | $25 | N/A | ✅ YES |
| Ambassador referrals | $50 | $20 | 2.5 months | ❌ NO (rework) |
| Organic referrals | $0 | $15 | N/A | ✅ YES |
| Label partnerships | $0 (barter) | $20 | N/A | ✅ YES |
| Reddit communities | $500 (swag) | $12 | Long | ❓ MAYBE |
| Facebook ads | $3 | $8 | 4+ months | ❌ NO |

**Rule:** CAC < (LTV / 3). If not, don't scale that channel.

## Network Effect Metrics

**Viral Coefficient:** Each user brings ___ new users

- < 1.0 = Death spiral (exponential decline)
- 1.0 = Flat (growth = new signups only)
- 1.1-1.3 = Sustainable growth (each user pays for themselves + profit)
- 1.5+ = Exponential growth (hockey stick)

**Goal by end of Phase 2:** Viral coefficient of 1.2+ (each user brings 1.2 friends)

## Anti-Patterns We'll Avoid

1. **Paid user acquisition before product-market fit** → Wastes money
2. **Vanity metrics** → MAU instead of DAU, downloads instead of active users
3. **Building for algorithm instead of for users** → Creates toxicity, not growth
4. **Chasing features instead of optimizing behaviors** → Bloat, not focus
5. **Ignoring churn** → Pouring water into a leaky bucket
6. **Growth theater** (big launches, press, hype) → Doesn't move the needle
